%Throughout this paper, the uppercase subscripts $F$ and $S$ are used to denote fluid- and solid-region quantities, respectively, and a trailing subscript $0$ denotes evaluation in the initial (reference) configuration. Where no ambiguity arises, the region subscript is omitted for conciseness.
Throughout this paper, bold symbols denote vectors and tensors (e.g.\ $\bb{v}_F$, $\bb{\sigma}_S$), while a hat denotes a unit vector (e.g.\ $\hat{\bb{n}}_F$). The uppercase subscripts $F$ and $S$ are used to denote fluid- and solid-region quantities, respectively, and a trailing subscript $0$ denotes evaluation in the initial (reference) configuration. Where no ambiguity arises, the region subscript is omitted for conciseness. The gradient operator $\bb{\nabla}$ is treated as a standard vector operator, consistent with the divergence $\bb{\nabla} \cdot$, curl $\bb{\nabla} \times$ and related operations, as adopted in OpenFOAM; with this convention, $(\bb{\nabla} \bb{a})_{ij} = \partial a_j / \partial x_i$ for a vector field $\bb{a}$, which is the transpose of the gradient tensor in the more usual continuum-mechanics convention. The subscript $0$ on the gradient, $\bb{\nabla}_0$, indicates differentiation with respect to the initial (reference) coordinates. Body forces are expressed per unit mass throughout.

The present work considers the dynamic interaction between an incompressible Newtonian fluid and a deformable elastic solid, as illustrated in Figure~\ref{fig:fsi_problem}.
At time $t$, the coupled system occupies the
moving domain
\begin{equation}
    \Omega(t) = \Omega_F(t) \cup \Omega_S(t),
\end{equation}
where $\Omega_F(t)$ and $\Omega_S(t)$ denote the fluid and solid domains,
respectively. Their boundaries are denoted by $\Gamma_F(t)$ and
$\Gamma_S(t)$, with outward-facing unit normals $\hat{\bb{n}}_F$ and
$\hat{\bb{n}}_S$. The fluid--solid interface is the common boundary
\begin{equation}
    \Gamma_\text{fsi}(t)
    =
    \Gamma_F(t) \cap \Gamma_S(t),
\end{equation}
on which the fluid and solid satisfy kinematic compatibility and dynamic
traction equilibrium.

The solid is formulated in a total Lagrangian description with respect to its
initial configuration $\Omega_{S,0}$, while the fluid is formulated on the
current moving domain $\Omega_F(t)$ using an arbitrary Lagrangian--Eulerian
(ALE) description. For notational compactness, the explicit time dependence of
moving domains and boundaries is omitted where no ambiguity arises.

\subsection{Solid model} \label{sec:solid_model}
%%--------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------%%

The motion of the solid is governed by conservation of linear momentum. In the
current configuration, this may be written in integral form as
\begin{equation}
    \int_{\Omega_S}
    \rho_S
    \frac{\partial \bb{v}_S}{\partial t}
    \, d\Omega_S
    =
    \oint_{\Gamma_S}
    \hat{\bb{n}}_S \cdot \bb{\sigma}_S
    \, d\Gamma_S
    +
    \int_{\Omega_S}
    \rho_S \bb{b}_S %\bb{f}_{b,S}
    \, d\Omega_S ,
    \label{eqn:solid_momentum_current}
\end{equation}
where $\rho_S$ is the current density, $\bb{v}_S$ is the solid velocity,
$\bb{\sigma}_S$ is the true (Cauchy) stress tensor, and $\bb{b}_S$ is a body force per unit mass.
% $\bb{f}_{b,S}$ is a body force per unit current volume.

The solid equations are solved in total Lagrangian form. Let $\bb{D}_S$ denote the solid displacement from the initial configuration
$\Omega_{S,0}$ to the current configuration $\Omega_S(t)$.
The deformation gradient and its determinant are
\begin{equation}
    \bb{F}
    =
    \bb{I}
    +
    \left( \bb{\nabla}_0 \bb{D}_S \right)^T,
    \qquad
    J = \det \bb{F},
    \label{eqn:deformation_gradient}
\end{equation}
where $\bb{\nabla}_0$ denotes differentiation with respect to the initial solid coordinates.
The transpose reflects the gradient convention stated in the introduction to this section.
%The transpose in Equation~\ref{eqn:deformation_gradient} arises from the chosen convention $(\bb{\nabla}_0 \bb{D}_S)_{ij} = \partial D_{S,j}/\partial X_i$, which treats $\bb{\nabla}_0$ as a standard vector operator consistent with $\bb{\nabla}_0 \cdot$, $\bb{\nabla}_0 \times$ and related operations, as adopted in OpenFOAM. With this convention, $\bb{\nabla}_0 \bb{D}_S$ is the transpose of the displacement gradient tensor in the more usual continuum-mechanics convention.
%With the convention
%$(\nabla_0 D_S)_{ij} = \partial D_{S,j} / \partial X_i$,
This corresponds in index notation to
\begin{equation}
    F_{ij}
    =
    \delta_{ij}
    +
    \frac{\partial D_{S,i}}{\partial X_j}.
\end{equation}

Using Nanson's relation,
\begin{equation} \label{eqn:nanson}
    \hat{\bb{n}}_S \, d\Gamma_S
    =
    J \bb{F}^{-T} \cdot \hat{\bb{n}}_{S,0} \, d\Gamma_{S,0},
\end{equation}
and introducing the first Piola--Kirchhoff stress tensor $\bb{P}_S = J \bb{\sigma}_S \bb{F}^{-T}$, the solid momentum equation may be expressed over the initial configuration as
\begin{equation}
    \int_{\Omega_{S,0}}
    \rho_{S,0}
%    \frac{\partial^2 \bb{d}_S}{\partial t^2}
    \frac{\partial \bb{v}_S}{\partial t}
    \, d\Omega_{S,0}
    =
    \oint_{\Gamma_{S,0}}
    \hat{\bb{n}}_{S,0} \cdot \bb{P}_S^T
%    \left(
%    J \bb{F}^{-T} \cdot \hat{\bb{n}}_{S,0}
%    \right)
%    \cdot
%    \bb{\sigma}_S
    \, d\Gamma_{S,0}
    +
    \int_{\Omega_{S,0}}
    \rho_{S,0} \bb{b}_S
    %\bb{f}_{b0,S}
    \, d\Omega_{S,0} .
    \label{eqn:solid_momentum_TL}
\end{equation}
Here $\rho_{S,0}$ is the initial density, and the reference-configuration traction is $\bb{t}_0 = \hat{\bb{n}}_{S,0} \cdot \bb{P}_S^T$.
The Cauchy stress $\bb{\sigma}_S$ (and hence $\bb{P}_S$) is supplied by the chosen mechanical law; in this work, the considered laws include linear elasticity and two finite-strain hyperelastic laws (neo-Hookean and Saint Venant--Kirchhoff).

The solid boundary conditions considered are prescribed displacement, prescribed traction, and symmetry conditions; interface conditions coupling the solid to the fluid are described in Section~\ref{sec:fsi_interface}.

\subsection{Fluid model}
\label{sec:fluid_governing_eqn}
%%--------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------%%

The fluid is assumed to be laminar, incompressible, Newtonian and isothermal.
It is formulated on the current moving domain $\Omega_F(t)$ using an arbitrary Lagrangian--Eulerian (ALE) description. Let $\bb{v}_F$ denote the fluid velocity and let $\bb{v}^{\Gamma}$ denote the velocity of the moving fluid domain.
The motion of $\Omega_F(t)$ is prescribed on the fluid--solid interface by the kinematic condition in Section~\ref{sec:fsi_interface}; its extension into the
interior of the fluid domain is a numerical mesh-motion procedure described in Section~\ref{sec:fluid_mesh_motion}.

For an incompressible fluid, conservation of mass over a moving control volume may be written as
\begin{equation}
    \frac{d}{dt}
    \int_{\Omega_F}
    d\Omega_F
    +
    \oint_{\Gamma_F}
    \hat{\bb{n}}_F
    \cdot
    \left(
    \bb{v}_F - \bb{v}^{\Gamma}
    \right)
    \, d\Gamma_F
    =
    0 .
    \label{eqn:fluid_continuity_ale_full}
\end{equation}

The moving domain is required to satisfy the geometric conservation law:
\begin{equation}
    \frac{d}{dt}
    \int_{\Omega_F}
    d\Omega_F
    -
    \oint_{\Gamma_F}
    \hat{\bb{n}}_F
    \cdot
    \bb{v}^{\Gamma}
    \, d\Gamma_F
    =
    0 ,
    \label{eqn:gcl}
\end{equation}

Subtracting Equation~\eqref{eqn:gcl} from Equation~\eqref{eqn:fluid_continuity_ale_full}, the mass conservation equation reduces to
\begin{equation}
    \oint_{\Gamma_F}
    \hat{\bb{n}}_F
    \cdot
    \bb{v}_F
    \, d\Gamma_F
    =
    0 .
    \label{eqn:fluid_continuity_ale}
\end{equation}

%In the finite-volume discretisation, this condition is enforced through the
%discrete velocity fluxes, with the mesh-motion contribution treated through the
%ALE relative flux.

For an incompressible Newtonian fluid with kinematic pressure $p$, the kinematic stress tensor is defined as
\begin{eqnarray}
    \bb{\sigma}_F^*
    &=&
    2 \nu \; \text{sym}
    \left[
    \bb{\nabla} \bb{v}_F
    \right]
    -
    p_F \bb{I} \notag \\
    &=&
    \nu
    \left[
    \bb{\nabla} \bb{v}_F
    +
    \left( \bb{\nabla} \bb{v}_F \right)^T
    \right]
    -
    p_F \bb{I},
    \label{eqn:fluid_stress}
\end{eqnarray}
where $\nu$ is the kinematic viscosity, and $\text{sym}[ \bb{A} ] = \frac{1}{2} (\bb{A} + \bb{A}^T)$ denotes the symmetric part. The true (Cauchy) stress is recovered as $\bb{\sigma}_F = \rho_F \bb{\sigma}_F^*$.
Following standard incompressible CFD practice, the pressure $p_F$ used throughout this paper is the kinematic pressure (the physical pressure divided by the fluid density $\rho_F$).
Conservation of linear momentum in ALE form is written as
\begin{equation}
    \frac{d}{dt}
    \int_{\Omega_F}
    \bb{v}_F
    \, d\Omega_F
    +
    \oint_{\Gamma_F}
    \hat{\bb{n}}_F
    \cdot
    \left(
    \bb{v}_F - \bb{v}^{\Gamma}
    \right)
    \bb{v}_F
    \, d\Gamma_F
    =
    \oint_{\Gamma_F}
    \hat{\bb{n}}_F
    \cdot
    \bb{\sigma}_F^*
    \, d\Gamma_F
    +
    \int_{\Omega_F}
    \bb{b}_{F}
    \, d\Omega_F ,
    \label{eqn:fluid_momentum_ale}
\end{equation}
where $\bb{b}_{F}$ is a body force per unit mass.

%    +
%    \left( \bb{\nabla} \bb{v}_F \right)^T
%    \right]
%    -
%    p \bb{I},
%    \label{eqn:fluid_stress}
%\end{equation}
%where $\nu$ is the kinematic viscosity and $p$ is the kinematic pressure.
%The true (Cauchy) stress $\bb{\sigma}_F = \rho_F \bb{\sigma}_F^*$ is the kinematic Newtonian stress tensor scaled by the fluid density $\rho_F$.

The fluid boundary conditions considered are inlet, outlet, no-slip wall and symmetry conditions; interface conditions coupling the fluid to the solid are described in Section~\ref{sec:fsi_interface}.

\subsection{Interface conditions}
\label{sec:fsi_interface}
%%--------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------%%

The fluid and solid are coupled by kinematic and dynamic conditions on the fluid--solid interface $\Gamma_\text{fsi}(t)$.
The kinematic condition enforces continuity of velocity between the fluid and solid and also prescribes the motion of the fluid-domain boundary:
\begin{equation}
    \bb{v}_F
    =
    \bb{v}_S
    =
    \bb{v}^{\Gamma}
    =
    \frac{\partial \bb{D}_S}{\partial t}
    \qquad
    \text{on } \Gamma_\text{fsi}(t),
    \label{eqn:kinematic_interface}
\end{equation}
%where $\bb{v}^{\Gamma}$ is the velocity of the moving fluid domain.
This condition enforces no-slip at all times; combined with consistent initial conditions, it also prevents gap formation or overlap at the interface.
Away from the interface, the extension of the boundary motion into the fluid domain is a numerical mesh-motion procedure and is described in Section~\ref{sec:fluid_mesh_motion}.

The dynamic condition enforces equilibrium of the fluid and solid tractions,
\begin{equation}
	\boldsymbol{t}_F + \boldsymbol{t}_S = \boldsymbol{0} \qquad \text{on } \Gamma_\text{fsi}(t),
	    \label{eqn:dynamic_interface_compact}
\end{equation}
with
\begin{equation}
	\boldsymbol{t}_F = \hat{\boldsymbol{n}}_F \cdot \boldsymbol{\sigma}_F = \rho_F \; \hat{\boldsymbol{n}}_F \cdot \boldsymbol{\sigma}_F^* ,
	\qquad \boldsymbol{t}_S = \hat{\boldsymbol{n}}_S \cdot \boldsymbol{\sigma}_S,
	\qquad \hat{\boldsymbol{n}}_F = -\hat{\boldsymbol{n}}_S.
\end{equation}
Equivalently, in terms of the kinematic fluid stress,
\begin{equation}
	\rho_F \; \hat{\bb{n}}_F \cdot \bb{\sigma}_F^* = -\hat{\bb{n}}_S \cdot \bb{\sigma}_S \qquad \text{on } \Gamma_\text{fsi}(t).
\end{equation}
