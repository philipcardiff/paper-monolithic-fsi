**One-paragraph summary (under 120 words):** This review surveys three decades of finite volume methods for computational solid mechanics. It classifies methods by grid arrangement, solution algorithm, and stabilisation strategy, then compares implicit cell-centred, implicit vertex-centred, explicit Godunov-type, and staggered-grid formulations. The method section derives dynamic linear elasticity in strong integral form, describes cell-centred displacement discretisation, Rhie-Chow-type stabilisation, segregated and block-coupled solution procedures, and explicit conservation-law formulations. The paper is relevant as a foundation for cell-centred FV solid mechanics and OpenFOAM-based solid solvers, but it is not a new FSI algorithm and does not present monolithic FSI, JFNK, PETSc, or an approximate block preconditioner.
